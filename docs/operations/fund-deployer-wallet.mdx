The mainnet program deploy described in
[Mainnet deploy runbook](/operations/mainnet-deploy) requires the
deployer wallet to hold **at least 6 SOL** before the
`solana program deploy` transaction is broadcast. A fresh ~200 KB
program costs roughly **5.5 SOL** in rent-exempt storage, plus a thin
margin for the IDL publish transaction and any redeploy attempts.

This runbook is the manual flow the human operator follows to put those
6 SOL in the right wallet, using Coinbase as the fiat onramp and
Phantom as the destination wallet. Fabric never touches money, never
sees the seed phrase, and never holds a key that can sign on mainnet
(premissa **IV.14** — no custody — and **V.16** — mainnet gating).

## When to use this runbook

Run this **once**, before the first mainnet deploy. After the initial
~5.5 SOL rent is paid, redeploys (program upgrades) cost only the
transaction fee, so the same wallet stays funded for the lifetime of the
program — top it up periodically rather than re-running the full flow.

You are doing this if:

- The operator WhatsApp ping from `scripts/notify-mainnet-ready.sh`
  fired and you do not yet have a Phantom wallet with ≥ 6 SOL on
  mainnet-beta.
- You are rehearsing the launch checklist (Z22) end-to-end on a clean
  wallet.
- The original deployer wallet was rotated post-incident and the new
  wallet needs initial funding.

If the deployer wallet already shows ≥ 6 SOL on
[solscan.io](https://solscan.io), skip this runbook and proceed straight
to `bash scripts/deploy-mainnet.sh`.

## Prerequisites

| Item | Why |
| --- | --- |
| Coinbase account, KYC complete | Premissa **V.17** — KYC stays at the onramp, not on us |
| ~$200 USD available (card, ACH, or PIX via Coinbase BR) | Covers ~6 SOL at typical mainnet pricing plus the network fee |
| Phantom mobile or browser extension installed | Premissa **I.5** — Phantom is the reference human wallet |
| A fresh, dedicated wallet inside Phantom | Do **not** reuse a personal wallet; the deployer key only ever signs deploys |
| 10 minutes uninterrupted | Coinbase 2FA, withdraw screen, and confirmation can stall a non-focused session |

<Warning>
The deployer wallet's seed phrase **never leaves the operator's
machine**. Do not paste it into Fabric, do not store it in 1Password
shared vaults, do not screenshot it. Premissa **IV.14** assumes Fabric
genuinely cannot sign — that assumption breaks if the seed is anywhere
Fabric can read.
</Warning>

## Step 1 — Create the dedicated Phantom wallet

Open Phantom and create a brand-new wallet **only for mainnet
deploys**. Keeping deploy-signing isolated from any personal balance
means a compromised browser session on your day-to-day wallet cannot
sign a malicious upgrade.

1. Open the Phantom extension (or mobile app).
2. Click the account avatar in the top-left → **Add / Connect Wallet**
   → **Create new wallet**.
3. Name it `zettapay-deployer-mainnet` so it shows up unambiguously in
   the account switcher.
4. Confirm the network selector reads **Solana** and the cluster reads
   **Mainnet** — not Devnet (Phantom defaults to Mainnet; verify
   anyway).
5. Write the 12-word seed phrase on paper, in a sealed envelope, in a
   physical safe. No cloud. No photo. No password manager unless it is
   a hardware-only vault.
6. Reveal the wallet's **Solana address** by tapping the account name
   at the top of the Phantom home screen → **Copy address**.

📸 *Screenshot to capture: Phantom home screen for the new
`zettapay-deployer-mainnet` account, with the **Copy address** button
visible. Save as `docs/operations/img/phantom-new-wallet.png`.*

You should now have a string that starts with a base58 character and is
~44 characters long. Paste it into a scratch note — you will need it
in Step 4. The address is public and safe to share.

## Step 2 — Sign in to Coinbase and pick the SOL market

1. Sign in at [coinbase.com](https://www.coinbase.com) on the same
   machine you will withdraw from. (Mobile works too, but the desktop
   view shows the withdraw fee more clearly.)
2. Complete any pending 2FA / device verification prompts. Coinbase
   sometimes blocks first-time large withdrawals; surfacing the prompt
   now avoids stalling at the withdraw screen.
3. In the top search bar type `SOL` and pick **Solana (SOL)**.
4. Confirm the price chart resolves and the **Buy / Sell** panel is
   visible on the right.

📸 *Screenshot to capture: Coinbase Solana market page with the
**Buy / Sell** panel on the right. Save as
`docs/operations/img/coinbase-sol-market.png`.*

## Step 3 — Buy ~$200 of SOL

1. Click **Buy**.
2. In the amount field type `200` (USD). Confirm the secondary line
   shows roughly the SOL amount you expect — at typical mainnet
   pricing this should be **5.5 – 7 SOL**. If the conversion shows
   less than 5 SOL, the SOL price has spiked; buy more (target 6 SOL
   net of fees) or wait.
3. Pick the payment method:
   - **Card** — instant, ~3.99% fee. Fine for one-off launch funding.
   - **ACH bank transfer** — cheaper, 1-3 days. Use only if you are
     pre-funding more than a week ahead of deploy.
   - **PIX (Coinbase Brasil)** — instant, low fee. Preferred for
     BR-based operators.
4. Review the **fees** line on the order preview. Coinbase will show
   a "you receive" amount that is **net** of fees. Confirm the
   **net SOL** is at least **6.0** — if not, increase the USD amount.
5. Click **Preview buy** → **Buy now**.
6. Wait for the order to fill. Card orders fill in seconds; ACH orders
   sit in `pending` until the transfer clears.

📸 *Screenshot to capture: Coinbase buy preview with the **net SOL**
line and the **fees** line both visible. Save as
`docs/operations/img/coinbase-buy-preview.png`.*

After the fill, the SOL balance on your Coinbase account page should
match the previewed net amount. If Coinbase shows it as `pending`,
**stop here** until the row clears to `completed` — Coinbase will not
let you withdraw a pending balance.

## Step 4 — Withdraw SOL to the Phantom wallet

1. From the SOL asset page on Coinbase, click **Send / receive** (top
   right) → **Send**.
2. In the destination field paste the Phantom address you copied in
   Step 1.6.
3. **Network** must be **Solana** — not "Solana on Ethereum" or any
   bridged variant. Premissa **I.1** — Solana is the chain; any other
   network at this step is wrong and the funds will land somewhere the
   deployer cannot reach.
4. In the amount field type the **full balance minus 0.05 SOL** (the
   margin covers Coinbase's network fee plus rounding). For a 6.3 SOL
   balance, send `6.25`.
5. Coinbase shows a confirmation screen with:
   - **Recipient address** — verify the first 4 and last 4 characters
     match what Phantom displayed. Coinbase will autocomplete an
     address book entry; do not trust the label, verify the raw bytes.
   - **Network fee** — typically ~0.000005 SOL on Solana mainnet.
     Coinbase passes the network fee through; their own fee should be
     `$0`.
   - **You will send** vs **Recipient will receive** — these should
     match within rounding.
6. Click **Send now**. Approve the 2FA prompt.

📸 *Screenshot to capture: Coinbase send-SOL confirmation screen with
the Phantom address, **Network: Solana**, and the 2FA prompt visible.
Save as `docs/operations/img/coinbase-send-confirm.png`.*

<Warning>
Address re-verification is the single highest-leverage step in this
runbook. A wrong address on Solana is **irreversible** — there is no
chargeback, no support escalation, no recovery. Always verify the
first 4 and last 4 characters of the destination address against
Phantom's display before approving the 2FA.
</Warning>

## Step 5 — Confirm the funds landed on-chain

Solana mainnet finality is sub-second, so the withdraw typically
appears in Phantom within **30 – 90 seconds**.

1. Open Phantom on the `zettapay-deployer-mainnet` wallet. The SOL
   balance should now match the withdrawn amount.
2. Cross-check on a block explorer in case Phantom is stale:

   ```bash
   solana balance <phantom-address> --url https://api.mainnet-beta.solana.com
   ```

   Or open
   `https://solscan.io/account/<phantom-address>` in a browser and
   confirm:
   - The latest transaction is the inbound transfer from Coinbase.
   - The post-balance is ≥ 6 SOL.
3. If after 5 minutes the funds have not arrived, check
   [status.solana.com](https://status.solana.com) — mainnet validator
   congestion can delay confirmation. Coinbase shows a transaction
   signature on the withdraw history page; paste it into
   `https://solscan.io/tx/<signature>` to see exactly where it is.

📸 *Screenshot to capture: Phantom home screen on the deployer wallet
showing the **≥ 6 SOL** balance on mainnet. Save as
`docs/operations/img/phantom-funded.png`.*

## Step 6 — Export the keypair for the deploy script

`scripts/deploy-mainnet.sh` consumes a filesystem keypair (per the
script's `MAINNET_DEPLOYER_KEYPAIR` env var). Phantom can export the
private key for an account in a format the `solana-cli` accepts.

1. In Phantom, click the account avatar → **Account details** →
   **Show private key**. Authenticate with the wallet password.
2. Copy the base58 private key string.
3. On the deployer machine (the isolated host that holds nothing else
   sensitive — **not your laptop**), run:

   ```bash
   solana-keygen recover prompt:// --outfile /secure/path/keypair.json
   ```

   When prompted for the seed phrase, paste the 12-word phrase
   instead — `solana-keygen` writes a JSON keypair file that
   `solana program deploy` and Anchor both accept.
4. `chmod 600 /secure/path/keypair.json` and store the directory on
   an encrypted volume.
5. Export `MAINNET_DEPLOYER_KEYPAIR=/secure/path/keypair.json` and
   proceed to [Mainnet deploy runbook §3](/operations/mainnet-deploy).

<Note>
If the deployer machine has a Ledger or another hardware wallet, prefer
that over an on-disk keypair. The Anchor and `solana-cli` flags accept
`usb://ledger?key=0` in place of `--keypair`. The on-disk keypair flow
documented above is the **minimum-viable** path for a one-off launch;
upgrade to hardware before any subsequent redeploy.
</Note>

## Cost & timing summary

| Item | Cost | Time |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Coinbase buy fee (card) | ~$8 on a $200 order (3.99%) | &lt;1 min |
| Coinbase withdraw fee | $0 platform + ~0.000005 SOL network | &lt;1 min |
| Solana mainnet confirmation | sub-second to ~90s under load | up to 5 min worst case |
| Total wall-clock | — | **~10 min** |

A successful run leaves ~6 SOL in the Phantom deployer wallet, a
`/secure/path/keypair.json` ready for `solana program deploy`, and a
Coinbase transaction history row that doubles as the audit trail for
the funding event.

## Troubleshooting

**Coinbase blocks the withdraw with "address not verified".** Coinbase
sometimes requires whitelisting a destination address for 48 hours
before withdrawing large amounts. Add the Phantom address to
**Settings → Address book** at least 2 days before deploy day, or
withdraw in two halves (the first half unlocks the address for the
second).

**Withdraw confirmation never arrives in Phantom.** Open the Coinbase
withdraw history page, grab the transaction signature, and inspect it
at `https://solscan.io/tx/<signature>`. If the signature shows
`Success` but Phantom does not update, Phantom is stale — pull to
refresh, or switch to a different account and back.

**Phantom shows a much lower SOL price than Coinbase.** Phantom uses
its own price feed (Pyth). Small drifts are normal; large drifts
(> 5%) usually mean Phantom is on an RPC endpoint that is lagging.
Switch RPC under **Settings → Developer Settings → Solana RPC URL**
to `https://api.mainnet-beta.solana.com` and re-open the wallet.

**Order is stuck on `pending` for hours (ACH).** ACH transfers can
take 1-3 business days to clear; SOL itself is held by Coinbase but
not withdrawable until the fiat side settles. Plan ACH funding at
least 5 business days ahead of the deploy slot.
